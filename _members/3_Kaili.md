<p> Kai Li is a Research Assistant under the Dynamic Brain & Memory Lab. She received her Bachelor of Social Sciences (Honours) in Psychology from NTU in 2025. </p>

<p> An aspiring researcher in cognitive neuroscience, Kai Li has explored various domains of psychological and neuroscience research; and has also worked in applied settings. </p> She completed her Graduation Thesis, <i>Assessing the development of cognitive control in preterm and full-term infants</i> with Baby-LINC lab. Additionally, an undergraduate project she completed, <i>The effect of maternal history of physical & emotional neglect on negative parenting practices </i>received 3rd place in the Discover-URECA poster competition.</p>

<p>She has completed internships in defence psychological research with DSO National Laboratories, psycholinguistic research with B.L.I.P Lab, and mental health with the Institute of Mental Health. She was also a long-term Student Assistant with the Centre for Research in Child Development. </p>

<p>Kai Li's unique blend of experiences has driven her interest to understand the constructs of memory and learning, and how we they may be modulated to support human thriving. Presently, she has been focused on integrating novel NLP methods into her methods.</p>
